1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

The importance of IT in modern business has a wide rage of purpose for any businesses

Digital marketing is more efficient than high cost newspaper television, and radio advertising.
Social networking is more efficient than going to clubs.
VoiP communication is more efficient than legacy telephone.
Cloud computing is more efficient than a private computer network.

The IT revolution has given business the tools to solve complex problems. For example, with the improvement of hardware combining smart applications it has made it easier to research data, analyse it and plan the growth of the business. There are many tools available and they will be discussed on 1.2.

IT allows business to make better decisions. For example, IT companies utilizing this technology are able to gain great market research. Online market research can be done in many ways and will be discussed in 1.2.

IT has been a massive influence to improve marketing. Using market research allows companies to target certain audiences, helping to discover their needs and building a marketing plan and campaign to persuade target audiences to buy.

IT has improved customer support. For example, as a company you have a many range of channels to offer customer support: telephone, email, website, social media and many more. This can help to improve customer relationships as having a fast and effective support system can increase repeat purchasing and improve customer advocacy.

IT has improved resource management. Cloud computing allows a company and its employees to use any device anywhere in the word to access their information and software.

lock General Data Protection Regulation(GDPR)

GDPR include 7 key principles:

  1. Lawfulness

  2. Fairness

  3. Transparency

  4. Purpose limitations

  5. Data minimisations

  6. Accuracy storage limitations

  7. Integrity and confidentiality

  8. Accountability

Compliance with these 7 key principles is a fundamental part for good data protection practice. It is also key to compliance with the detailed provisions of the GDPR. Failure to comply with these principles opens a person or persons to substantial fines. This could include a fine of up to $20 million euros or 4% of your total worldwide annual turnover, whichever is higher.
